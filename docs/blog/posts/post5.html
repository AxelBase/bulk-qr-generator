PNG vs SVG: Which QR Format Should You Choose?

PNG vs SVG: Which QR Format Should You Choose?

Published: November 22, 2025

After generating 5,000 QR codes, you get two download buttons: PNG ZIP and SVG ZIP. But which one should you actually use?

PNG – Raster (Pixel-Based)

  • File size: ~4–12 KB per 400px QR
  • Works everywhere: email, WhatsApp, websites, PowerPoint
  • Fixed resolution — don’t enlarge too much
  • Best for digital distribution

SVG – Vector (Scalable)

  • File size: ~1–3 KB per QR
  • Infinitely scalable — print at poster size with zero quality loss
  • Editable in Illustrator, Inkscape, Figma
  • Perfect for stickers, badges, signage, embroidery

Real-World Recommendations

Use CaseRecommended Format
Event tickets (email/PDF)PNG
Name badges & lanyardsSVG
Product labels & packagingSVG
Website & social mediaPNG
Vinyl stickers & bannersSVG

Pro Move: Download Both

The tool lets you download both formats at once — no extra work. Keep PNGs for digital use and SVGs for print vendors.

When in doubt: generate both. Storage is cheap. Regret is expensive.
