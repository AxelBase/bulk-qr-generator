Custom Filenames, Prefixes, and Colors

Custom Filenames, Prefixes, and Colors

Published: November 22, 2025

Generic QR codes named qr_00001.png work — but they’re not professional. Your attendees, customers, and inventory managers expect better.

This tool gives you full control over naming and appearance — directly from your CSV.

Smart Filename Mapping

Have a column called “Ticket ID”, “Asset Name”, or “Attendee”? Just select it as the Filename column and every QR code will be saved exactly as that value.

Example CSV:

name,url
VIP-John-Doe,https://event.com/vip/123
Table-42,https://event.com/table/42

Resulting files:

  • VIP-John-Doe.png
  • Table-42.png

Files are automatically sanitized (including spaces and special characters safely handled).

Automatic Prefix & Suffix

Stop manually editing URLs. Add a prefix like https://mystore.com/ and a suffix like /redeem once — every QR content becomes:

https://mystore.com/ABC123/redeem

Perfect for ticketing systems, affiliate links, or WiFi passwords.

Full Visual Branding

Match your brand in seconds:

  • Size: 200×200 to 500×500 px
  • Error correction: L to H (survives damage or logos)
  • Foreground & background colors (color picker)

Want black-on-transparent for stickers? White-on-navy for business cards? Done.

Pro Tip: Combine Everything

CSV column “code” → prefix https://check.in/ → suffix /vip → filename from “name” column → red foreground → 400px → SVG output.

One click. Thousands of perfectly branded, uniquely named, scannable QR codes.

Your QR codes should reflect your brand — not look like everyone else’s.
