Understanding QR Code Error Correction Levels

Understanding QR Code Error Correction Levels

Published: November 22, 2025

QR codes can survive damage — scratches, dirt, partial obstruction — thanks to Reed-Solomon error correction. But not all QR codes are equal.

This tool lets you choose between four levels: L, M, Q, and H. Here’s what they actually mean.

The Four Levels Explained

LevelRecovery CapacityData OverheadBest For
L (Low)~7%Least modulesClean environments, maximum data
M (Medium)~15%Good balanceDefault — recommended for most uses
Q (Quartile)~25%High redundancyIndustrial, outdoor, small print
H (High)~30%Most modulesLogos, stickers, harsh conditions

Real-World Damage Tests

A QR with 30% H-level correction can be scanned even if:

  • A large logo covers the center
  • 25% of the code is scratched or torn
  • It’s printed on curved or textured surfaces
  • It’s partially covered in dirt or snow

Level L fails at ~8% damage. Level H survives triple that.

When to Use H (and When Not To)

Use H when: printing stickers, badges, product labels, or adding logos.
Use M when: digital display, clean paper tickets, or controlled environments.

Trade-off: Data Capacity

Higher correction = fewer data modules = less URL capacity at same physical size. A 400px H-level QR holds ~15–20% less data than L-level.

For branded or durable QR codes, always choose Q or H. The resilience is worth the small data trade-off.
